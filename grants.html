Grants Available

You may be eligible for a grant from the Sustainable Energy Authority Ireland (SEAI) when purchasing a battery-powered electric vehicle (BEV) that meets specific standards. As of July 1, 2023, the grant amount for BEVs has been adjusted to a maximum of €3,500, reduced from the previous €5,000. This grant is applicable exclusively to new cars within the price range of €14,000 to €60,000. Your car dealer will handle the grant application on your behalf, deducting the approved grant amount from the total price of your BEV. It's important to note that, effective January 1, 2022, the grant of €2,500 for plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEV) has been discontinued.

Charging Grants

You also have the option to participate in the Home Charger Grant Scheme, which offers financial support of up to €600 to assist with the installation expenses of a home charging station. This scheme aims to encourage and facilitate the adoption of electric vehicles by providing assistance for the setup of convenient charging infrastructure at your residence. By applying for this grant, you can enhance the accessibility and convenience of charging your electric vehicle at home, contributing to the broader initiative of promoting sustainable and eco-friendly transportation choices

Toll Reductions

Owners of electric vehicles have the opportunity to benefit from reduced toll fares under the Low Emissions Vehicle Toll Incentive (LEVTI) Scheme. The refund amount granted through the scheme is contingent upon various factors such as the vehicle type, class (private, light goods vehicle, SPSV, and HDV), toll location, and the time of passage. To avail of this incentive, it is essential to acquire an EV tag. The scheme is anticipated to remain in operation until 31 December 2023 or until it reaches a usage threshold of 50,000 drivers, whichever comes first.
